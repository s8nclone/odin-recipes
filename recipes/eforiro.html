Efo Riro

Description

Efo riro is a vegetable soup and a native soup of the Yoruba people of western Nigeria.
The two vegetables most commonly used to prepare the soup are Celosia argentea and Amaranthus hybridus.
Its place of origin is the Yorubaland (Western Nigeria)

Ingredients

  • smoked fish
  • scotch bonnets (atarado)
  • tatashe (red bell pepper)
  • onions
  • crayfish
  • water
  • palm oil
  • red onion
  • leaf vegetables
  • locust beans
  • seasonings
  • meat(cow skin, cow tripe)
  • Salt

Steps

  1. season the meats, add sliced onions and boil on medium heat
    Just before its fully cooked, add the Stockfish, boil till tender and set aside
  2. Wash your vegetables thoroughly with hot water to rid it of all traces of dirt
  3. Slice or chop according to your preference and set aside.
  4. Blend the scotch bonnets and bell peppers and an onion coarsely and set aside
  5. Into a large pot, add the palm oil, leave to heat up on medium heat for 2 minutes
  6. Then add the locust beans, fry to release the flavour for another minute
  7. Now add the blended pepper and two seasoning cubes, let that fry for 15-20 minutes
  8. Add a bit of the meat stock/ chicken stock, if you haven't got stock,
    just add water, be careful not to add too much
  9. Let it boil for 2-3 minutes to combine, then add the assorted meats, prawns, crayfish,
    stockfish and catfish and leave for another 10 minutes
  10. Now add the washed vegetables, combine thoroughly
  11. Switch off the heat at this time, leave to simmer for a further 5 minutes with the residual heat
  12. Food is ready
  13. Serve with any “swallow” of your choice. Efo Riro is also prefect with white rice, boiled yam and plantain.
